About Me

I'm a proven engineer who has a decade of experience in the automotive manufacturing industry. I've been working at Denso, the second largest parts and systems supplier in the world, since 2013. I've been promoted several times since then and I'm currently a Design Engineer III. I started at Denso as a mold design engineering co-op when I was a junior in college. When I graduated a year later from college, I quickly hit the ground running, learning the ins and outs of Siemens NX design software. I have designed tunes and implemented customer design changes on hundreds of molds. From running FEA with Moldex3D to managing new mold builds and running mold trials, I have a broad range of skills to accomplish any task. Through simulation, I have optimized several molding parameters such as gate location and fill pattern. I've successfully incorporated additive technology, designing dozens of 3D printed steel inserts. This resulted in saving tens of thousands of dollars in mold maintenance and cycle time.

I've gotten into data science the past couple years. I realize the impact it is having on the world, and I want to be involved. At Denso, I've collected cooling fan warp data from the past twenty years and applied data science to understand how different product design features affect the warp. I was able to apply my warp prediction model to new cooling fans and accurately predict warp, saving tens of thousands of dollars in tuning costs.

My passion is problem solving and optimization. These things excite me because it enables me to use my creative juices and troubleshoot until I find the most effective solution.
